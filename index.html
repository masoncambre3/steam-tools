# Startup Condensate Load Calculator Calculates warm-up condensate load from bringing cold steam piping to saturation temperature. Primary use case is drip leg trap sizing, where startup load (not running load) is the governing condition. Part of the Steam Field Tools suite. ## Method Q = W x cp x (Tsat - Tstart) / (hfg x t) - Q = mean condensate load (lb/h or kg/h) - W = total pipe metal weight - cp = 0.12 BTU/lb-F (0.49 kJ/kg-C) for carbon steel - t = warm-up time in hours Design load = mean load x safety factor (1x to 3x, selectable). 2x typical for supervised startup, 3x for unsupervised. ## Data - Pipe weights: ASME B36.10 carbon steel, NPS 1/2" to 24", Sch 40 and Sch 80 - Steam properties: saturated steam table interpolation, 0 to 600 psig - Imperial and SI units with in-place conversion ## Scope Pipe metal only. Valve bodies, flanges, and fittings are excluded. For headers with heavy valves, add their mass manually or step up the safety factor. ## Deployment Single self-contained HTML file, no dependencies. Serves as index.html for Vercel.
